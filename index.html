Chapter 1: Force and Pressure

Force is a push or pull that can change the state of rest or motion of an object. Its SI unit is Newton (N).

Effects of force: move a stationary object, stop a moving object, change speed/direction, change shape.

Types of forces: Contact (muscular, friction, normal) and Non-contact (gravitational, magnetic, electrostatic).

Pressure = Force / Area. Smaller area → more pressure (e.g., sharp knife). Atmospheric pressure is due to air weight; helps in drinking straw, rubber sucker.

Numerical example: A force of 100 N acts on an area of 0.5 m². Pressure = 100/0.5 = 200 Pa.

Chapter 2: Friction

Friction is the force that opposes relative motion between two surfaces. It is caused by surface irregularities.

Types: Static (largest), Sliding (medium), Rolling (smallest). Ball bearings convert sliding into rolling friction.

Ways to reduce friction: Lubricants (oil, grease), polishing, wheels, streamlining. Increase friction: Treads on shoes/tyres, sand on ice.

Fluid friction (drag): streamlined shapes reduce drag (fish, aeroplanes).

Example: A box of 50 kg requires 300 N to slide on rough floor, but only 100 N on ice – less friction.

Chapter 3: Sound

Sound is produced by vibrations. It needs a medium (solid, liquid, gas) – cannot travel in vacuum. Speed of sound is highest in solids.

Characteristics: Amplitude (loudness), Frequency (pitch) – human audible range 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz. Ultrasound >20 kHz, Infrasound <20 Hz.

Human ear: Pinna collects → ear canal → eardrum vibrates → middle ear bones amplify → inner ear converts to electrical signals → brain.

Noise pollution causes health issues. Music is pleasant, regular vibrations.

Chapter 4: Chemical Effects of Electric Current

Conductors: allow electricity to pass (metals, graphite, salt solutions). Insulators: do not allow (rubber, plastic, pure water).

When electric current passes through a conducting liquid, chemical reactions occur – electrolysis. Example: water splits into H₂ and O₂.

Electroplating: coating one metal on another using electric current. Uses: rust prevention (zinc on iron), decoration (chromium, silver).

Key fact: Pure water is a poor conductor; adding salt or acid makes it conduct due to ions.

Chapter 5: Some Natural Phenomena

Lightning: electric discharge between clouds or cloud and earth. Caused by charge separation. Safety: stay indoors, avoid water/electrical appliances, don't stand under trees.

Earthquake: sudden shaking due to movement of tectonic plates. Focus (origin inside earth), Epicentre (point on surface). Measured by seismograph; magnitude on Richter scale.

Safety during earthquake: Drop, Cover, Hold. Stay away from windows, heavy objects.

Static electricity: charging by rubbing (comb + paper).
