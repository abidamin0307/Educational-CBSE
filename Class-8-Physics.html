Chapter 1: Force and Pressure

Force is a push or pull that can change the state of motion or shape. SI unit: Newton (N).

Effects of Force

  • Move a stationary object
  • Stop a moving object
  • Change speed/direction
  • Change shape

Types of Forces

Contact ForcesNon‑contact Forces
Muscular, Friction, NormalGravitational, Magnetic, Electrostatic

Pressure = Force / Area → smaller area = larger pressure (e.g., sharp knife).

Atmospheric pressure – air around us exerts pressure (straw, sucker).

Chapter 2: Friction

Friction opposes relative motion. It is caused by surface irregularities.

  • Static friction – largest, prevents motion.
  • Sliding friction – opposes sliding.
  • Rolling friction – smallest (wheels, ball bearings).

Increasing friction: treads, sand. Reducing friction: lubricants, polishing, wheels.

Fluid friction (drag) – streamlined shapes reduce drag.

Chapter 3: Sound

Sound is produced by vibrations. Needs a medium (solid, liquid, gas) – cannot travel in vacuum.

Amplitude → loudness. Frequency (Hz) → pitch. Human audible range: 20 Hz – 20,000 Hz.

Human ear: outer ear collects → eardrum vibrates → middle ear amplifies → inner ear converts to signals.

Chapter 4: Chemical Effects of Electric Current

Conductors allow electricity (metals, salt solutions). Insulators do not (rubber, plastic, pure water).

Electrolysis: chemical decomposition by electric current. Electroplating: coating one metal on another using current (rust prevention, decoration).

Chapter 5: Some Natural Phenomena

Lightning: electric discharge between clouds or cloud & earth. Safety: stay indoors, avoid water/wires, no open field.

Earthquake: sudden shaking due to tectonic plate movement. Focus, epicentre. Safety: Drop, Cover, Hold.

Seismograph records earthquakes; Richter scale measures magnitude.
